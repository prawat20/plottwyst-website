What is the Plottwyst?

Every case contains a Plottwyst — a deliberate misdirection built into the evidence from the very first clue. The opening clues point confidently in the wrong direction. The red herring has a motive, an opportunity, and seemingly damning evidence stacked against them.

Your job isn't just to find the murderer.
It's to see through the lie.

⚠️ "The opening clues may not point where you think. Trust the evidence — but question what it's really telling you."

How a Case Unfolds

1

The Case is Revealed

A unique murder case is generated — victim background, 6 suspect profiles each with a motive, personality, and alibi statement, plus 3 opening clues to start the investigation.

2

Discuss the Evidence

Your team debates theories, challenges alibis, and cross-references clues against profiles. A timed discussion phase keeps things moving — use the time wisely.

3

Vote to Clear Suspects

Each round, vote to eliminate the suspect you're confident is innocent. 4 rounds, one elimination per round. But vote out the actual murderer and the killer escapes immediately.

4

Name the Murderer

After 4 rounds, every detective makes one final private guess. Then the truth is revealed — the killer's identity, their real motive, and exactly how the Plottwyst was built to fool you.

About Plottwyst

Plottwyst is a cooperative murder mystery game built for Discord. A crime has been committed, and your team has to solve it — studying suspects, gathering evidence across 4 rounds, and naming the murderer before the trail goes cold.

Every case is uniquely generated. Different victim, different suspects, different motives, different setting. No two investigations are ever the same.

The Plottwyst

The name says it all. Every case contains a Plottwyst — a deliberate misdirection built into the evidence from the very first clue.

The opening clues don't just hint at a red herring. They build a complete, convincing case against the wrong suspect. Motive, opportunity, and seemingly damning physical evidence all point at someone who didn't do it. The real killer is hidden in plain sight.

Experienced players can't meta-game their way through it. The misdirection changes every case — sometimes it targets an obvious suspect, sometimes it clears the murderer entirely. The only way through is careful reasoning.

How It's Built

Plottwyst runs entirely inside Discord using slash commands and interactive buttons. There's nothing to install, no accounts to create, and no separate app to open. Add the bot, type /lobby, and your first case starts in under a minute.

Cases are generated across multiple genres — Victorian England, 1920s noir, modern corporate thrillers, and more. Each genre has its own atmosphere, vocabulary, and style of misdirection.

Currently in Beta

Plottwyst is currently in open beta. The core game is fully playable — lobbies, suspect profiles, voting, final guesses, leaderboards, and the full resolution reveal all work today.

Features in development:

  • Private DM clues — each detective receives unique private evidence alongside the shared clues
  • Murderer Among Players — one player secretly plays as the killer
  • Custom game settings — control round timing, difficulty, and genre
  • Premium tier — more players per game and unlimited daily games

Beta feedback directly shapes what gets built next. If something feels off about a case — the misdirection didn't land, the clues were confusing, the resolution didn't make sense — the post-game feedback buttons send that signal directly.
